## Cuban domestic law (on the ground)
- Law 118/2014 permits 3 forms of foreign investment: **empresa mixta (JV)**,
  **contrato de asociación económica internacional (AEI)**, **empresa de capital
  totalmente extranjero (wholly-foreign-owned)**. [P] — MINCEX Ley 118 PDF.
- Free transfer abroad of dividends/profits in freely convertible currency, **no
  transfer tax**. [P]
- **No expropriation** except declared public-utility/social-interest by the
  Council of Ministers, with indemnification at commercial value in convertible
  currency. [P]
- Open in **all sectors except** health & education services to the population and
  the armed institutions (except their enterprise systems). Opportunities published
  by MINCEX in the **Cartera de Oportunidades**, approved by the Council of
  Ministers. [P]
- Regime anchored by **Law 118/2014 + Decreto 325/2018** (reglamento). [P]
- **Acuerdo 8732/2019** delegated AEI-contract authorization to the heads of
  Central State Administration bodies. [P]
- **Decreto-Ley 46/2021 (MIPYMEs) was REPEALED and replaced by Decreto-Ley
  88/2024.** [P] — Gaceta Oficial.
- 2026 Cuban migration/investor reform exists (Granma 2026-05-06; elToque) but a
  reform letting **foreign/diaspora take direct equity in MIPYMEs** could **not** be
  verified against primary law. [?] — treat as unconfirmed.

## U.S. sanctions law
- **§515.340** defines "independent private sector entrepreneur": a Cuban national,
  NOT a prohibited Government/Party official, who owns/works a small private
  business, cooperative or sole proprietorship in Cuba of **≤100 employees**.
  [P] — Fed. Reg. 2024-11618 (May 29 2024) + OFAC FAQ.
- **§515.582** amended ("Cuban"→"private sector") to authorize **importing goods &
  services** from independent private sector entrepreneurs. [P] State processing
  that yields a new name/character/use disqualifies; packing-only or export-agent
  state involvement is OK. [P]
- **§515.584(h)** (new 2024): U.S. banks may **open/maintain accounts solely in the
  name of** a Cuban independent private sector entrepreneur. [P]
- (Ingested corpus) Remittances **§515.570**, internet-based services **§515.578**,
  telecom/payments **§515.542** are the other authorizing GLs.
- **GAESA** designated under **EO 14404 on May 7 2026** — a new IEEPA-based program
  separate from the CACR. [P] GAESA on the SDN + Cuba Restricted List since **Dec 21
  2020**; U.S. persons prohibited from transacting unless OFAC-authorized. [P2]
- **Wind-down to June 5 2026** for *foreign* persons winding down GAESA dealings;
  does **not** authorize any CACR-prohibited transaction for U.S. persons. [P2]
- EO 14404 SDN block on all GAESA property; sector authority (finance/mining/
  energy/defense/security); FFI secondary-sanctions exposure. [S] — Akerman, MoFo.
- **Helms-Burton Title III** (treble-damages "trafficking" suits) broadened by the
  Supreme Court's **Havana Docks** decision (May 21 2026); **Title IV** visa bars for
  principals of trafficking entities. [S] — Jones Day, Skadden, Sullivan & Cromwell.

## The bottom line
No OFAC general license authorizes **U.S.-person equity** in Cuban private
enterprises, and the Cuban-side "diaspora equity" opening is unverified. The only
clearly-authorized lane is **remittance/payment support** to ≤100-employee
independent private entrepreneurs via channels that avoid GAESA/FINCIMEX/state
banks — i.e. the QvaPay support model. Stand up any structure only with OFAC +
securities counsel. Not legal advice.
